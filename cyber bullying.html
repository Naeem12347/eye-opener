CYBER BULLYING AND PRIVACY

Creating a Safe and Respectful Digital Environment at School AT KINGS COLLEGE BUDO, we believe every student has the right to learn, grow, and connect in a safe and respectful environment both in the classroom and online. As part of our commitment to student well-being, we actively educate and support our school community around the important issues of cyber bullying and online privacy. What is Cyber Bullying Cyber bullying is when someone uses technology like a phone, computer, or social media to deliberately hurt, harass, embarrass, or exclude another person. It can happen through text messages, comments on posts, private messages, or even gaming platforms.

Examples of cyber bullying:

  • Sending threatening or mean messages
  • Spreading rumours or lies online
  • Posting embarrassing photos or videos without permission
  • Excluding someone from group chats or activities on purpose
  • Creating fake profiles to bully or impersonate others

Cyber bullying can be just as harmful as fac-to -face bullying and often more difficult to escape. Why Privacy Matters Your personal information like your full name, school, photos, phone number, or location should be protected. When shared online, even “privately” it can be misused or spread without your consent. Protecting your privacy helps :

  • Keep you safe from online bullies
  • Prevent identity theft or hacking
  • Stop personal content from going viral
  • Reduce the risk of online threats or blackmail

How Cyber bullying Affects Students

Cyber bullying can have serious impacts on a student’s mental health, friendships, learning and self-esteem. It may cause;
  • Stress, anxiety, or depression
  • Avoidance of school or activities
  • Sleep or concentration problems
