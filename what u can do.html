What can you do ?

Students:

  • Think before you post. Would you say it in person?
  • Don’t share passwords with friends.
  • Use privacy settings on apps and social media.
  • Block and report bullies, don’t respond or retaliate.
  • Tell a trusted adult like a parent, teacher, or counsellor if something feels wrong.

Parents

  • Talk regularly with your child about their online activity
  • Set clear expectations around device use and privacy
  • Encourage open conversations about any problems online.
  • Support your child if they experience or witness cyber bullying

Teachers and Staff:

  • Be alert to signs of distress that may relate to online issues
  • Foster classroom discussions about respectful online behaviour
  • Respond quickly and consistently to reports of cyber bullying.

Report It. Don’t Ignore It. If you or someone you know is being cyber bullied:

  • Report it to a teacher, counsellor, or school leader
  • Keep screenshots or evidence
  • Block the bully and avoid responding
  • Talk to someone you trust
